Time and Space Complexity Analysis

Functions Individual Time Complexity Individual Space Complexity
addItem() O(nlogn) O(n)
searchItem() O(m) O(1)
updateItem() O(n) O(1)
deleteItem() O(n) O(1)
allocateNumberPlate() O(1) O(1)
Dijkstra() O((V+E)logV) O(V)

Time Complexity:

  • For functions with O(nlogn) (e.g., addItem()), this dominates other linear terms like O(n) in updateItem() and deleteItem() for larger values of n.
  • O((V+E)logV) in Dijkstra() is significant for graph-related operations, and it could dominate depending on the graph size V and E.

The overall complexity can be expressed as:

T.C = O(max(nlogn, n+m, n, 1, (V+E)logV))

  • For large n, O(nlogn) dominates O(n).
  • For graph-related operations, O((V+E)logV) might dominate O(nlogn), depending on the size of V and E.

Resulting Overall Time Complexity:

  • If the graph-related operation (Dijkstra()) is used frequently and V and E are larger than n, the overall complexity will be: O((V+E)logV)
  • Otherwise, for inventory operations with no significant graph tasks: O(nlogn)

Space Complexity:

Combining Space Complexities:

The space complexity is determined by the function with the highest space requirement:

O(max(n, m, V))

  • If V > max(n, m), the space complexity is O(V).
  • Otherwise, it is O(n) (e.g., for inventory operations).

Therefore,

Overall Time Complexity: O(max(nlogn, (V+E)logV))

Overall Space Complexity: O(max(n, V, m))
